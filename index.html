What's really happening

He's not apologizing — he's apologizing for your feelings while denying any responsibility. Phrases like "you always do this" and "you're overthinking" are designed to make you doubt your own perception. Suggesting therapy shifts focus to you being the problem. This is a textbook deflection strategy.

Emotional impact on you

You likely feel confused, blamed, and slightly ashamed — as if bringing this up was the mistake. That's exactly the intended effect. Your reaction was valid. His response was not.

✦ Suggested reply (calm & confident)

"I hear that you're frustrated. But telling me I'm overthinking instead of addressing what I said isn't working for me. I need us to actually talk about this, not dismiss it."
